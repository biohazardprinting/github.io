About the Owner and Biohazard Printing

Allow me to introduce myself - I'm Derek, the mastermind behind Biohazard Printing, a -year-old resident of northern Ohio. Nestled in this cozy corner of the world, I navigate the intricacies of life alongside my wife and kids, juggling the responsibilities of family, work, and my ever-expanding passion project.

Now, how did this wild ride begin, you ask? Well, picture this: a regular guy with a full-time gig, just tinkering away in his spare time, turning random ideas into tangible creations. That was me, until one fateful day when lightning struck and those creations morphed into something more than just a hobby. Suddenly, I found myself on the cusp of a new adventure, one brimming with endless possibilities and unforeseen twists.

But wait, rewind a bit. How did I come up with the name Biohazard Printing? Well, gather 'round and let me share the tale. Several years ago (seems like forever ago) I served in the United States Army as a 74D, a Chemical, Biological, Radiological, and Nuclear Specialist. Don't let the title get ya, it's a fancy title for saying you're there to clean up a BIG mess. From those military days, the concept of biological warfare stuck with me, intertwined with memories of donning protective gear and navigating new territories. Thus, the name "Biohazard" was born, inspired by my military experiance.

As fate would have it, 3D printing emerged as the cornerstone of my newfound venture. With every nozzle pass of the printer, my imagination ran wild, sculpting wonders out of mere plastic. Since 3D printing was the first big thing my business did, that's how the "Printing" name came into play, and alas, Biohazard Printing.

But the adventure didn't stop there. As my business grew, so did my repertoire of skills. I have since ventured into laser engraving, custom apparel, custom images, and even website building. With each new endeavor, I discovered new passions and honed my craft, pushing the boundaries of what Biohazard Printing could achieve.
